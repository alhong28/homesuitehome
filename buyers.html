Buyers

Quick Tips For A Successful House Hunt Write down features that your future home must have and try to stick to it. If you are shopping with your partner, craft this list together so you’ll have a clear picture of the dream home during your search.

Not only are you buying a home, but you’re also buying the neighborhood around it. Is it safe? Does your life fit into the community? Talk to neighbors and ask questions. Spend time in the area to determine if it offers the lifestyle and conveniences you’ll need.

Don’t overwhelm yourself by seeing every house on your list in one day. Making the most important purchase decision in your life requires patience and poise. Take it one house at a time and note which properties check off more boxes than the rest.

Whatever you do, DO NOT BLOW YOUR BUDGET. Young buyers want what they see on TV, but it’s not always wise to buy the biggest and most expensive house you can afford at an early age. On top of expenses such as taxes, home owner’s association fees, and maintenance, you’ll want money left over for Friday nights with friends, vacations to Europe, and savings for your rainy day fund.

Rent Or Buy? Mortgage rates are at a historic low, and investors have been taking advantage of cheap money. You’ve probably noticed dozens of luxury high-rise apartments being built in your area. It may be tempting to hand over rent money for a nice studio and call it a day, but one thing is certain: rent will climb like clockwork every year. Compared to buying your home and making the same payment every month, renting is a rat race. Remember that you can make money on real estate in 4 different ways, whereas you can surely lose money by renting.

Steps To Take To Prepare To Buy A Home Within A Year If you are planning to purchase within the year then your priority right now should be to clean up your credit. Pull those reports, see if there is anything that may make the process difficult. Any past dues? Any lates? Judgements? Call the creditors and work out a deal. The key to getting a good mortgage rate is credit worthiness.

Banks offer your mortgage rates based on your credit score, so you can save yourself thousands of dollars in interest by having good credit. Pull your score today to make sure that nothing stands in the way between you and your future home. Clear up any past due medical bills, high credit card balances, or unpaid student loan debt. While shopping for homes, avoid sinking your money into other debt, such as taking a loan out for a new car or a closet full of shoes that you put on credit. Remember that lenders issue mortgages based on your debt to income ratio. You can maximize your purchasing power by minimizing debt.

Make sure that your taxes are in order. Lenders usually need your last 2 tax returns so have that ready before you talk to a mortgage professional.

Lastly, save as much money as you can. Most people think that you just need the down payment and the seller will hand you the keys. You’ll also need to pay attorney, inspection, and closing fees. After you close, you’ll want money in reserve to fix small issues around the house or remodel it to fit your wants.
