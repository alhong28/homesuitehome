Why Invest in Real Estate?

Unlike other investments, real estate provides 4 ways in which you can make money. Spoiler alert: you can’t see most of the money that makes it into your pocket. Compared to stocks and bonds, real estate provides far greater benefits at the cost of liquidity.

Cash Flow - your tenants will pay you rent every month, regardless if you had to do any work to the property or not. This is the most obvious profit center, and the most controllable. Because we never want to pay out of pocket to keep an investment afloat, we should always invest for cash flow. Make sure that the rental income covers your mortgage payment, maintenance and repairs to the property, property management to automate your business, and then some so you can save for your next investment.

Market Appreciation - historically, real estate has appreciated 5% on average year-over-year. There are upswings and downswings in the market, such as the financial crisis in 2007, but overall the market always recovers, especially when your property is positioned in a highly desirable area. Location, location, location! You can also make improvements to your home, also called forced appreciation, such as repainting the interior, updating the kitchen, and refinishing the bathroom. By matching what similar properties in your neighborhood have, you can increase the value of your home by thousands with a few simple touchups.

Equity Position - every month that you pay the mortgage, you’ll gain equity in the house. Once you get to the end of your mortgage term (typically 30 years), you’ll own the house free and clear. Pro tip: pay half of the mortgage twice a month to artificially knock a few years off your loan term. Staying on top of this every month will save you thousands in interest payments. Once you have enough equity in a property, you have the right to take out a home equity line of credit (HELOC). This will allow you use your current property as collateral to buy another investment. See how this creates a snowball effect?

Tax Savings - as a business owner, you’re eligible to deduct any expenses associated with operating your business on your tax return. This includes any repairs or improvements (but not additions), vehicle miles to and from the property, and professional fees like property management. You can also take advantage of the 1031 exchange, which is one of the most powerful tax shelters allowed for real estate. When you sell a stock and you’ve made money on it, you’re required to pay capital gains tax at the highest rate. The opposite is true in real estate. This IRS law stipulates that if you sell your existing property, identify a replacement property within 45 days, and purchase another property within 180 days, all capital gains are deferred and you pay NO TAXES. If you do this continuously and keep trading up to larger property every year, you’ll never pay capital gains tax on it. You can start with a $50,000 single family home and end up with a $50 million apartment building without owing the IRS a dime.

Automate or DIY?

Landlording can be fulfilling at times, but it can also come at a great cost. When you’re a landlord, you also sign up to be the leasing agent, handyman, accountant, inspector, and attorney. For most people, having to unclog a toilet is more than enough reason to avoid investing in real estate. Having the right property manager can solve all of your problems. By paying just a fraction of your rental income, you can sit back and watch rent checks be automatically deposited into your bank account every month. Your job as an investor is not to patch up drywall or re-caulk the shower. Your job is to find the next investment and expand your real estate empire. When using my Real Estate Evaluator tool, property management is already added into the equation so you know which homes can pay for this service.

What is an FHA loan?

An FHA loan is a mortgage that's insured by the Federal Housing Administration (FHA). This is a powerful investment tool because it allows down payments of 3.5% for buyers with credit scores of 580+. For example, if you purchase a multi-family property for $500,000, your down payment is only $17,500 excluding closing costs. Because of the low initial investment, your return on investment will be artificially increased. The more leverage you use, the more money you can make.

However, there are several disadvantages to using an FHA loan. First, a transaction involving FHA typically takes a month longer than a traditional sale. This happens because FHA will need to send its own appraiser out (your mortgage lender will do this too) before approving your loan. Moreover, FHA stipulates that you must live on the property as your primary residence for at least 1 year. Afterwards, you can move out into another property that you buy. Additionally, borrowers must pay mortgage insurance premiums (MIP), which protects the lender if a borrower defaults. This usually adds several hundred dollars to your expenses and lowers your bottom line. On top of this, paying such a low down payment means that your monthly cash flow will also be lower than if you took out a conventional loan. This is usually not a problem, because you would have used my Real Estate Evaluator tool to calculate a healthy positive cash flow. Lastly, you can only take out 1 FHA loan at a time. If you want to utilize this tool for another property, you’ll need to reach 20% equity on your existing loan first.

By knowing all of the loan products that are available on the market, you can pick the one that fits your investing style. Talk to one of our preferred mortgage professionals to learn more about how you can use this tool to leverage your way into your next big investment.

What is a HELOC?

HELOC stands for home equity line of credit. Unlike a home equity loan, a HELOC allows the borrower to use only part of the available amount. You can use this to pay off high-interest credit cards, finance a home improvement project, or even to leverage your way into the next investment property. Remember that your rental properties gain equity every month the mortgage is paid, so your ability to buy the next investment snowballs with each additional property. This compounding effect allows you to purchase investments with little to no money down if you exercise patience. Another great benefit of taking out a HELOC is that the interest may be tax-deductible. Be sure to ask your tax professional for more information before using this investment tool. On the other hand, an investor should be aware that HELOCs are typically variable rate. Be sure to ask your mortgage professional if the rate offered is a discounted rate or an introductory rate.
